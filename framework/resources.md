# Resource Directory: Global Standards & Cognitive Accessibility

This directory bridges global academic research with practical toolkits to ensure emergency information is reachable, readable, and resilient.

---

## 1. Global Design & Media Accessibility Research

These resources from the **GDA (Global Design for All)** community focus on the intersection of media, technology, and universal design.

### GDA 2024: Book of Abstracts

**Link:** [GDA 2024 Book of Abstracts](https://webs.uab.cat/gda/wp-content/uploads/sites/316/2024/03/GDA-%E2%80%93-Book-of-abstracts-7.pdf){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Useful For:** Deep dives into "Media Accessibility" and "Digital Government" as a human right.

**Who it's for:** Researchers, digital strategists, and accessibility architects.

**Why it matters:** It contains the latest academic thinking (2024-2026) on how to move from "standard" accessibility to "universal" digital participation.

### Global Design for All (GDA) 2023 Conference

**Link:** [GDA 2023 Conference](https://webs.uab.cat/gda/gda-2023/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Useful For:** Reviewing high-level discussions on "Design for All" in a post-pandemic, climate-uncertain world.

**Who it's for:** Policy makers and emergency management leadership.

**Why it matters:** The conference presentations specifically address how technology can empower—or exclude—vulnerable populations during societal shifts.

## 2. Plain Language & Easy Read Standards

*Cognitive accessibility is the most ignored aspect of disaster prep. These guides define the specific "Stress-Ready" layouts required.*

### ASAN: One Idea Per Line Guide

**Link:** [One Idea Per Line](https://autisticadvocacy.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/One-Idea-Per-Line.pdf){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Useful For:** A specific visual template for reducing cognitive load by isolating instructions to a single line.

**Why it matters:** This is the "survival layout" for neurodivergent individuals and those in psychological shock.

### 18F Content Guide: Plain Language Approach

**Link:** [18F Plain Language](https://guides.18f.org/content-guide/our-approach/plain-language/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Useful For:** Techniques for cutting "Bureaucratic-speak" to speed up information processing.

**Who it's for:** Government communications teams.

**Why it matters:** Direct, active-voice instructions save time and lives during rapid-onset disasters.

---

## 3. Digital Resilience & Sustainable Design

*Building for "Digital Famine" means respecting the limits of devices and networks during a crisis.*

### W3C Web Sustainability Guidelines (WSG) 1.0

**Link:** [WSG 1.0](https://www.w3.org/TR/web-sustainability-guidelines/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Useful For:** Implementing "Asset Shedding" and low-power UI to ensure sites load on 1% battery.

**Why it matters:** Aligning with WSG ensures that when the comms team sends people to your site during a flood, it actually loads on stressed towers.

### Glia: Open Source Medical Hardware

**Link:** [Glia](https://glia.org/){:target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"}

**Useful For:** Accessing 3D-printable designs for medical spares (tourniquets, otoscopes).

**Why it matters:** Provides a "Digital-to-Physical" bridge for medical infrastructure when supply chains are severed.

## 4. Implementation Matrix: The "Emergency Mode" Logic

Use this matrix to audit the resources you point to on your GitHub-hosted site:

| Pillar | Success Metric | Why it Matters in Crisis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Accessibility** | WCAG 2.2 / EN 301 549 | Ensures screen readers and switch-controls work during evacuations. |
| **Sustainability** | WSG 1.0 Compliance | Minimizes data/battery drain for users on "one-bar" cellular signals. |
| **Clarity** | Grade 6 Plain Language | Accounts for "temporary cognitive disability" caused by trauma. |
| **Persistence** | Permanent Archive.org Link | Ensures the information is available even if the source agency goes offline. |

## How to Use These Resources

### For Emergency Communications Teams

1. **Start with Plain Language:** Review the 18F and ASAN guides
2. **Check Technical Standards:** Use WCAG 2.2 and WSG 1.0 as checklists
3. **Review Academic Context:** Read GDA materials for strategic thinking

### For Policy Makers

1. **Understand the "Why":** Read GDA conference presentations
2. **Set Requirements:** Use the Implementation Matrix to define standards
3. **Monitor Compliance:** Regular audits against all four pillars

### For Developers

1. **Build to Standards:** WCAG 2.2 Level AA minimum
2. **Optimize for Crisis:** WSG 1.0 for low-bandwidth scenarios
3. **Test Resilience:** Verify content works offline and on old devices
